You have one week to select one of the following topics for your internship but not limited to:
# Topic Details
1 Evacuation planning in emergency situations In a volcano eruption or tsunami, an early warning for evacuation will help to save many lives. However, it is challenging to broadcast exact messages to hundreds of thousands of people to evacuate in a way:
(1) preventing the situation of many people going in a road and congestion occurring so slowing down the evacuation;
(2) broadcasting messages based on the user's location.
(3) Create a clear evacuation map for people to follow
Keywords: Google evacuation map, https://artsandculture.google.com/asset/the-evacuation-route-map-office-of-the-prime-minister/RQGRlNc0T68HZQ?hl=en
2 Signal surveillance in restricted-access areas To detect suspicious and bad guys in a restricted-access area without risking their life of direct physical intrusion, law enforcement may need a tool to monitor whether there are human activities in a specific building, detect the exit/entrance doors in the building or walkable areas. In this research, you will explore to use signal localization techniques to (1) draw the building shape from signal traces (2) predict the movement directions of the targets inside.

Keywords: Signal tracking, Object sensing
3 Specific brand IoT device detection In important networks, reliable devices come from trusted sources are a must to maintain the integrity of network traffic. However, due to cost, many citizens may not fully follow the regulation. They may still use several old devices or come from unreliable sources. This task is to detect IoT devices (smart speaker, smart lamp, smart bridge...) that come from specific countries or unreliable sources

Keywords: IoT device detection, IoT device identification

Refer example#: https://github.com/kahramankostas/IoTDevIDv2
4 Ransomware/Malicious attack/CVE vulnerabilities detection Currently, ransomware/CVE vulnerabilities are severe threats to many critical applications and network infrastructure. In this topic, we will explore new deep learning models to detect ransomware/malicious attacks in public dataset. Besides, the ultimate goal is to explore the ransomware network or injected chain (use MITRE ATT&CK)

Keywords: Intrusion detection system, DDoS attack detection, Ransomware detection

1. Ransomware dataset: https://github.com/manabu-hirano/RanSAP
2. IoT dataset https://www.stratosphereips.org/datasets-iot23 or https://www.unb.ca/cic/datasets/iotdataset-2023.html
5 Deep fake/AI-generated content detection Currently, deep fake is one of the biggest threats to civil applications or politic campaign. New AI technologies can enhance even further the authenticity of information. Detection content/images/videos generated by AI or deep fake videos is then a critical matter. In this topic, you will explore several techniques to detect the deep fake/AI-generate videos/images, e.g., by face swap, mouth swap

Keywords: Disinformation, deep fake detection, AI-generated content detection

Dataset: https://github.com/Daisy-Zhang/Awesome-Deepfakes
6 Integrated sensing and communications (ISAC) ISAC is one of three pillars for 6G. However, this technology is still at the early state of development. In this topic, you will explore methods to using ISAC to model the problem of detecting UAVs, smart phone's location in the environment without Internet

Keywords: cellular sensing, device-to-device sensing

Refer: https://github.com/PolyuWirelessCommunicationLab/Data-Association-in-Network-Device-Free-Sensing
7 Small vehicles in satellite images Currently, satellite images are popular in many applications, e.g., soil monitoring, rescue, and surveillance in conflicted areas. In this topic, you will explore SOTA techniques to classify the small objects in high-resolution satellite images and figure out specific object types with on-demand inputs.

Keywords: Small object detection, satellite image segmentation

Refer: https://github.com/kuanhungchen/awesome-tiny-object-detection or https://paperswithcode.com/task/small-object-detection
8 Quantum machine learning Quantum computing and machine learning are the emerging research topics to explore the powerful capability of these two technologies for enhancing AI
in digital twins and quantum networks . In this topic, you will explore some lightweight quantum ML to solve some conventional problems, e.g., Quantum Generative Adversarial Networks for Image Generation, PennyLane.

Keywords: Quantum machine learning, PennyLane

Refer: https://paperswithcode.com/task/quantum-machine-learning
9 AI-generated articles ChatGPT and AI tools are widely used in many fields. However, the biggest problem with their answers is the reliability where it is hard to know which information is correct, which part is fabricated information. In this topic, you develop a tool to detect an article or a paragraph whether it is generated by AI tools. The goal is to alert everyone to think twice in using those materials.

Keywords: AI-generated content, GPTZero ,

Refer: https://github.com/IBM/RADAR or https://github.com/allentsangdev/ai-text-detector
10 Computer vision for critical applications Computer vision and NLP have got many promising achievements in object detection, image segmentation, AGI,.. which is a part of the intelligent smart manufacturing and critical applications (smart grids, intelligent transportation..) In this topic, you develop a tool to solve the matters of critical applications, e.g., detect small defects in products, gesture-based authentication (hand gesture, face ID via camera, gait-based)
Keywords: CV-based object detection, gesture/biometric-based authentication
11 Computer vision-based authentication Cameras are widely used in public areas, including the buildings in CCU. In this application, you will develop a gesture-based authentication (hand gesture, face ID via camera, gait-based). For example when you enter a building, you don't need to use an ID entry card, an AI system at CCTV/cameras can detect your body gait, face, hand gesture to open the door. This can help much when we often forget ID card.

Keywords: gesture/biometric-based authentication
Refer: https://www.graphics.rwth-aachen.de/publication/02257/
